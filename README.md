# Yamuna Water Quality Monitor (2013 - 2024)

## 📊 Project Overview
This project leverages historical environmental datasets from the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) to evaluate the spatial and temporal ecological health of the Yamuna River. By tracking core chemical and biological parameters, this interactive Power BI dashboard identifies critical pollution channels, evaluates government intervention trends, and flags high-risk ecological dead zones within the urban Delhi corridor.

## 🔑 Key Environmental Insights Documented
* **Critical Organic Load:** The river's average Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) stands at **29.11 mg/l**, alarming at nearly 10 times the CPCB's maximum safe bathing limit of 3.00 mg/l.
* **Severe Microbial Contamination:** Total Coliform concentrations average **1.40 Billion** and Faecal Coliform sits at **2.84 Trillion**, exposing continuous unmitigated domestic sewage discharge.
* **Oxygen Depletion Phenomenon:** Visualized through an inverse scatter plot, demonstrating a direct correlation where high organic inflows (BOD breaching 40-50 mg/l) collapse Dissolved Oxygen (DO) levels to near 0 mg/l at the Nizamuddin and Okhla Barrages.
* **Chemical Ingress Indicators:** Identified structural household and industrial waste tracking via elevated levels of Surfactants (6.00 mg/l) and Phosphates (19.10 mg/l) bypassing municipal treatment infrastructures.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & DAX Methods
* **BI Tool:** Microsoft Power BI
* **Data Sources:** Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) Raw Monitoring Data (2013-2024)
* **Techniques Used:** Data Modeling, Spatial Hotspot Mapping, Advanced Scatter Plots (BOD vs DO), Custom HD Narrative Integration, Time-series trend profiling.

## 🚀 Recommended Actionable Solutions
Based on localized data drill-downs, generalized resource allocation across the river basin is inefficient. Remediation infrastructures must prioritize centralized Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) at the 4 major drainage bottlenecks: Gazipur Drain (Agra Canal), Shahdara Drain, Najafgarh Drain Confluence, and Okhla Barrage Zone.
